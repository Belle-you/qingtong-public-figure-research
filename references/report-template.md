# Report Template

Use this structure. Remove empty sections only when they are irrelevant, and state why.

## Title

`Public-Information Due-Diligence Report: [Name]`

## Scope And Limits

- Identity and disambiguation
- Decision context
- Research date and time range
- Jurisdictions and languages searched
- Explicit exclusions and limitations

## Executive Summary

In 3-6 bullets, summarize the strongest decision-relevant findings. Include confidence and status. Do not issue a moral verdict.

## Identity Check

Confirm that records refer to the correct person. Note aliases, organizations, and ambiguity.

## Evidence-Based Timeline

| Event date | Finding | Status / confidence | Sources |
|---|---|---|---|

Use event dates, not merely article publication dates.

## Risk Signals

Group by relevant risk category. For each signal include:

- Finding
- Why it matters to the stated decision
- Status and confidence
- Supporting evidence
- Counterevidence, response, or later outcome
- Remaining uncertainty

## Positive And Exculpatory Evidence

Include credible positive conduct, corrections, remediation, dismissals, and evidence that weakens negative claims.

## Conflicts And Gaps

List unresolved contradictions, missing records, paywalled or inaccessible sources, and searches that returned no reliable evidence.

## Decision Checklist

Translate the evidence into questions the user can answer:

- Does this cross an explicitly stated deal-breaker?
- Is the evidence strong enough for the consequence being considered?
- What should be independently verified before acting?
- Would a narrower or reversible next step reduce uncertainty?

## Sources

List direct links, publisher, publication date, and access date. Avoid citing search-result snippets.

## Language Patterns

Use:

- "The public record reviewed shows..."
- "According to [source], which directly supports..."
- "This allegation remains unresolved because..."
- "No reliable evidence was found in the sources searched; this is not proof of absence."

Avoid:

- "Obviously guilty"
- "Definitely trustworthy"
- "Everyone knows"
- "There is a 73% chance their values match yours"
